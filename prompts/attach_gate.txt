An EVENT is one named occurrence or process — a budget, an election, a court
case, a scheme, a disaster, a controversy — including its reactions, analyses,
follow-ups and consequences. An event is NOT a topic, a region, a party, or a
rivalry between people.

EVENT TITLE: {event_title}
EVENT SCOPE: {scope}
EVENT BEGAN: {event_began}
RECENT DEVELOPMENTS: {recent_milestones}

ARTICLE DATE: {article_date}
NEW ARTICLE: {new_title}
SUMMARY: {new_summary}

Does the new article fall within this event's scope?
- ATTACH if it reports a development, reaction, analysis or consequence of the
  occurrence named in the scope — even if it differs from earlier developments.
- REJECT if ARTICLE DATE is earlier than EVENT BEGAN — an article cannot
  belong to an event that had not yet begun.
- If the article's headline names a different specific occurrence (a trade
  deal, another state's budget, another court case), REJECT — shared
  ministers, Parliament sessions, or market impact do not override this.
- If in doubt, REJECT.

Answer with one word: ATTACH or REJECT
