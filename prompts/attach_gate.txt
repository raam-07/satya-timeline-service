An EVENT is one named occurrence or process — a budget, an election, a court
case, a scheme, a disaster, a controversy — including its reactions, analyses,
follow-ups and consequences. An event is NOT a topic, a region, a party, or a
rivalry between people.

Does the new article fall within this event's scope?
Decide step by step:
1. REJECT if the article is outside the scope: a different occurrence, a
   general roundup, or other actions by the same people, parties or regions.
2. REJECT if the article's headline names a different specific occurrence
   (a trade deal, another state's budget, another court case) — shared
   ministers, Parliament sessions, or market impact do not override this.
3. REJECT if the article is about a different occurrence even with the same
   people or similar economic themes. (e.g. a trade deal is not the Union
   Budget, even if both move markets and involve the Finance Minister.)
4. Only if none of the above apply: ATTACH if it reports a development,
   reaction, analysis or consequence of the occurrence named in the scope —
   even if it differs from earlier developments.
5. If in doubt, REJECT.

Answer with one word: ATTACH or REJECT
